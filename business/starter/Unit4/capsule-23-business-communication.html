Business Communication — Taking & leaving a message · Unit 4

The person you call is not there? No problem — leave a message. Someone calls your colleague? Take a message. Study the key expressions on the side panel. 👉

Model call STUDY
Bruna: Sales. Hello?
Diego: Hi. Is Camila there?
Bruna: No, I'm sorry, she's out. Can I take a message?
Diego: Yes, it's Diego in IT. I'm calling about the new sales report.
Bruna: Go ahead.
Diego: Please ask her to call me back. My extension is 4 0 2.
Bruna: Sorry, can you repeat that?
Diego: 4 0 2.
Bruna: So that's 4 0 2. Is there anything else?
Diego: No, that's all. Thanks!
Bruna: OK. I'll give Camila your message.

Activity 2 — What do you say? BY WRITTEN

Write the expression for each situation:

  1. You answer, and the caller wants your colleague. You offer to write the information. → "Can I ________ a ________?"
  2. You don't understand the number. → "Can you ________ that?"
  3. You want to confirm the number 517. → "________ ________ 5 1 7."
  4. You finish taking the message. → "I'll ________ her your ________."
Activity 3 — Role-play: take a message BY AUDIO

🎙️ Record a complete call — you do both voices: someone calls asking for Pedro (he's out), leaves a message about the meeting on Friday and a call-back number. Use: Can I take a message? · Can you repeat that? · So that's… · I'll give Pedro your message.

✍️ Complete Activities 1–2, then 🎙️ record your call (Activity 3).

Key Expressions — Messages
📝 Taking a message
Can I take a message?
Go ahead.
I'll give (Pedro) your message.
📞 Leaving a message
I'm calling about (the report).
It's about (the meeting).
Please call me back.
My number is (…).
🔁 Repetition & checking
Sorry, I don't understand.
Can you repeat that?
So that's (4 0 2).
👋 Ending
Is there anything else?
💡 Always repeat numbers back: "So that's…" — it avoids mistakes.
