Practically Speaking — Numbers 0–9 · Unit 2

At work you say numbers every day: phone numbers, flight numbers, codes, passwords. In English, we say phone numbers digit by digit (número por número).

The numbers STUDY

1one
2two
3three
4four

💡 Tip: in phone numbers, 0 is usually "oh". Double digits: double five = 55.
Activity 1 — Listen and repeat BY AUDIO

▶ Play the teacher's audio. Repeat each number after me — first slowly, then fast.

Activity 2 — Ditado (number dictation) BY WRITTEN

⚠️ The numbers are only in the audio. Listen and write what you hear:

  1. Flight number: GOL G3 1______
  2. Extension number: ______
  3. Mobile number: (21) 9______ - ______
  4. Door passcode: ______
Activity 3 — YOUR numbers BY WRITTEN + AUDIO

Complete the table with real numbers (or invent them):

  1. My work number is ______________
  2. My extension number is ______________
  3. My mobile number is ______________

Then 🎙️ record yourself saying the 3 numbers, digit by digit, in English.

✍️ Do the ditado (Activity 2) and write your numbers (Activity 3), then 🎙️ record them.
