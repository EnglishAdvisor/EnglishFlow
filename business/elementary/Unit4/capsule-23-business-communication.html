Business Communication — Asking for and offering help · Unit 4

Technical problem? 😱 Don't panic — ask for help! And when a colleague has a problem, offer help. Study the key expressions on the side panel. 👉

Model dialogue STUDY
Aline: Ugh… I can't log in to the file-sharing system.
Bruno: Do you want a hand?
Aline: Yes, please. I don't know how to reset the password.
Bruno: OK. Click on "Forgot password" and check your inbox.
Aline: How do I do that?
Bruno: Look — like this. Now enter the new password. Remember: it uses UPPER-CASE letters.
Aline: It works! That would be… I mean, that's great, thanks!
Bruno: You're welcome.
Activity 1 — A, O or R? BY WRITTEN

Is each expression Asking for help, Offering help, or Responding?

  1. Can you help me? ___
  2. Can I help? ___
  3. Yes, of course. ___
  4. Do you want a hand? ___
  5. How do I do that? ___
  6. That would be great, thanks. ___
  7. Can you give me a hand? ___
  8. Sure. ___

Key Expressions — Help
🙋 Asking for help
Can you help me?
Can you give me a hand?
How do I…?
I don't know how to…
🤝 Offering help
Can I help?
Do you want/need a hand?
✅ Responding
Yes, of course. · Sure.
Yes, please.
That would be great.
No, I'm OK, thanks.
🙏 Responding to thanks
You're welcome.
💡 "a hand" = ajuda (informal): Do you want a hand? = Quer uma ajuda?
