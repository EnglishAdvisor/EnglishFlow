📖 Open your Student's Book on pages 54–55: the National Geographic article "Mount Everest's Deadliest Day" — two teams compete to reach the summit first, with deadly results.
Before you read — Predict
Look at the title and photos ONLY (don't read the text yet!) and answer:
1. What do you think happened on Mount Everest that day?
2. What dangers do you imagine climbers face on Everest? List 3.
While you read — Comprehension
Now read the article in your book and answer in your own words:
1. Who are the two groups (teams) in the story, and what was the relationship between them?
2. What sequence of events led to the tragedy? Describe it in 3–4 sentences using time connectors (after, while, as soon as…).
3. In your opinion, what was the biggest mistake made that day — and by whom?
4. Find in the text: 2 words or phrases connected to mountains/climbing and 2 connected to danger/emergency. Write them with a short definition.
After you read — React 🎙️
Record yourself answering (1–2 minutes): "Should people continue to climb Everest, even though it is so dangerous? Why (not)? Would YOU ever try it?" Use vocabulary from this unit: ordeal, intense, handle, rescue, panic…

✍️ Write your Predict + Comprehension answers — and 🎙️ record your reaction. Send via WhatsApp.
