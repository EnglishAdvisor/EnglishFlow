The debate
⛰️ Local business people, mountain climbers and environmental groups are debating a big question:
Should Mount Everest be closed to visitors?
Some want to stop all climbing for safety and environmental reasons. Others say closing it will make many people lose their jobs.
Book reference: SB page 57. Your mission: hold the debate — and reach a compromise (an agreement where each side accepts part of the other's idea).
The 4 positions
🧗 Everest guide Guiding is your business and your team's jobs depend on it — but you have seen accidents happen, and some clients are not well trained.
🏔️ Mountain climber Climbing Everest is your life dream. You have trained for years and don't want the mountain to close — but you worry about inexperienced climbers.
🌿 Environmentalist Thousands of visitors mean pollution and garbage on the mountain. You believe it must close so that it can be cleaned up and preserved.
🏘️ Local guide Guiding pays well and supports your family — but you lost a relative in an avalanche near the summit. In your opinion, something has to change.
Your task 🎙️
1. Choose TWO roles with opposite opinions. 2. Write each one's position in 1–2 sentences. 3. Record a mini-debate (you do both voices!) where they discuss and reach a compromise. Use the strategy phrases from the side panel — at least one from each group.
💬 Model: "One option would be to close Everest for a year so that we can clean it up…"

✍️ Write your 2 positions + your final compromise — and 🎙️ record the mini-debate. Send via WhatsApp.

Speaking Strategy
💡 Making a suggestion
One option/idea would be to…
❌ Disagreeing
The problem with that is…
Sorry, but that doesn't work for me because…
✅ Agreeing
I could do that. / I agree to that.
That works for me.
🤝 Offering a compromise
If you'll agree to…, I'll agree to…
