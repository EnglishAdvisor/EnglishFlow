Adjective forms — too / enough / comparatives / superlatives
too + adjective / adjective + enough
The mud is too thin. (= not thick enough)
It isn't thick enough.
Is the derrick tall enough? (= is it the right height?)
It's too short. (= it isn't tall enough)
Comparatives (-er / more)
1-syllable adjectives: thick → thicker, short → shorter
2-syllable ending -y: noisy → noisier, heavy → heavier
Long adjectives: expensive → more expensive
The new rig is more efficient than the old one.
Superlatives (-est / most)
thick → the thickest | heavy → the heaviest
efficient → the most efficient
The derrickman has the most dangerous job on the rig.
