Key vocabulary for drilling
I know the main parts of a drilling rig: drill bit, drill string, derrick, kelly, rotary table, mud pump, mud tank, mud screen, cuttings, top drive.
Drilling crew roles
I can describe what roustabouts, roughnecks, the derrickman, driller, and toolpusher do.
Problem and solution conversations
I can describe a drilling problem and say why it happened and how to fix it (e.g. mud too thin → add clay).
Adjective forms — comparatives and superlatives
I can form and use comparatives (thicker, more efficient) and superlatives (the deepest, the most dangerous) correctly.
Silent /r/ in British English
I understand when the letter r is pronounced (r + vowel) and when it is silent (r + consonant or at end of word).
Giving safety advice
I can give safety advice using "You shouldn't … because … might …" and write instructions in the imperative form.
