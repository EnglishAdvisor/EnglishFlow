Giving safety advice — drilling situations
Useful structures
You shouldn't [do X] because [person / equipment] might [get hurt / be damaged].
You should [do Y] because it's safer.
Always [do Z] — never [do X] when [condition].
1
A roustabout is cleaning the floor with his hands instead of a mop.
2
A roughneck is not wearing gloves when connecting heavy drill pipes.
3
A worker is climbing the derrick without a safety harness.
4
Someone is standing directly under the travelling block while it is moving.
5
A worker is adding clay to the mud tank without a face guard.
6
The driller is operating the controls with the safety gate open.
7
A worker is running on the wet drilling floor.
8
A roughneck is disconnecting drill pipes without locking the hoist first.
9
Workers are not wearing hard hats while unloading pipe from a truck.
10
A worker is smoking near the mud tank and fuel storage area.
Example
Situation 3:
"You shouldn't climb the derrick without a safety harness because you might fall and get seriously hurt."
Key vocab
might — possibility: "you might fall"
should/shouldn't — advice
always/never — strong rules
harness — strap securing a worker
hoist — lifting device
face guard — face protection
hard hat — head protection
lock — secure before work
