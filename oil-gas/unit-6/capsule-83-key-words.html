adjective
thick
/θɪk/
Not flowing easily; dense in consistency.
The mud is too thick — the pump is struggling.
adjective
thin
/θɪn/
Flowing easily; not dense.
The mud is too thin to carry the cuttings up.
noun
derrick
/ˈderɪk/
Tall steel structure that supports the lifting equipment above the drill hole.
The derrick is 45 metres tall on this rig.
noun
derrickman
/ˈderɪkmæn/
Worker who operates high up on the derrick and looks after the mud equipment.
The derrickman works on the monkey board, 25 metres up.
noun
drill bit
/drɪl bɪt/
Toothed rotating device at the bottom of the drill string that cuts and crushes rock.
We need to replace the drill bit after every 500 metres.
noun
drill string
/drɪl strɪŋ/
Long string of connected pipes from surface to drill bit, rotating as it drills.
The drill string may contain 100 sections of pipe.
noun
motor
/ˈməʊtə/
Machine that drives the rotary table or top drive to rotate the drill string.
The drive motor is very powerful.
noun
mud
/mʌd/
Fluid mixture of water, clay, and additives pumped down the drill string to cool, lubricate, and carry cuttings.
The mud carries the rock cuttings to the surface.
noun
pump
/pʌmp/
Machine that forces the mud through the drill string under pressure.
If the pump is noisy, the mud is too thick.
noun
roustabout
/ˈraʊstəbaʊt/
Junior unskilled worker on the drilling crew; cleans and moves equipment.
Roustabouts start at the bottom of the drilling hierarchy.
noun
toolpusher
/ˈtuːlpʊʃə/
Most senior person on the drilling crew; responsible for safety and paperwork. Also called rig manager.
The toolpusher has 20 years of drilling experience.
verb
connect
/kəˈnekt/
To join two things together.
Roughnecks connect the heavy drill pipes one by one.
verb
disconnect
/ˌdɪskəˈnekt/
To separate two joined parts.
They disconnect the drill pipes as they come out of the hole.
verb
guide
/ɡaɪd/
To direct carefully into the right position.
The derrickman guides the top of the pipe up to the monkey board.
verb
tighten
/ˈtaɪt(ə)n/
To make something firm and secure, preventing movement.
Tighten all connections before starting the mud pump.
