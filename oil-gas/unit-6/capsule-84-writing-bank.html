Writing Bank 6 — Emails asking for leave
⚠️ Always ask for leave in advance. Be polite, clear, and include the dates. Use formal or semi-formal language depending on your workplace.
Email 1 — Asking for annual leave
To: supervisor@rigjobs.com Subject: Annual Leave Request — 14–21 July Dear Mr Santos, I would like to request annual leave from 14 July to 21 July (8 days). I have already completed my current assignments and my work is up to date. I would be happy to brief a colleague before I leave. I hope this request can be approved. Please let me know if you need any further information. Kind regards, Ahmed Al-Rashid Roughneck, Drilling Crew 4
Email 2 — Reporting sick leave
To: supervisor@rigjobs.com Subject: Sick Leave — [Your name] Dear Mr Santos, I am writing to inform you that I am unable to come to work today due to illness. I have a high fever and my doctor has advised me to rest for 2–3 days. I have a medical certificate which I will send to HR as soon as possible. I apologise for any inconvenience and will keep you updated on my condition. Best regards, [Your name]
Email 3 — Requesting emergency leave
To: supervisor@rigjobs.com Subject: Emergency Leave Request Dear Mr Santos, I am writing to request emergency leave due to a family emergency. My mother has been taken to hospital and I need to travel home urgently. I would like to request leave from tomorrow and I expect to return within 5 days. I am sorry for the short notice and will arrange for my duties to be covered. Yours sincerely, [Your name]
1Find the phrases — match function to email
1. Which email includes a reason for not coming to work?
✓ Email 2 (sick leave) — "I am unable to come to work today due to illness."
2. Which phrase shows the request is polite and formal?
✓ "I would like to request…" / "I hope this request can be approved."
3. What document does the sick worker promise to send?
✓ A medical certificate.
4. How does the emergency leave writer show professionalism?
✓ He apologises for short notice and offers to arrange cover.
5. What information is in the Subject line of Email 1?
✓ The type of leave + exact dates.
2Write your own email
Choose one of the three types of leave and write an email asking your supervisor. Use the templates above as a guide. Include: type of leave, dates, a reason, and a professional closing.

Leave vocabulary
annual leave — scheduled holiday days
sick leave — time off due to illness
emergency leave — urgent unplanned absence
medical certificate — doctor's note
in advance — before the event
apologise — say sorry
inconvenience — trouble caused to others
brief a colleague — explain your work to a colleague
Email structure
Subject — type + dates
Opening — Dear Mr/Ms…
Request — I would like to…
Reason — due to / because…
Dates — from X to Y
Closing — Kind regards /Yours sincerely
