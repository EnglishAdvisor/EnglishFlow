The oil and gas industry is one of the most hazardous industries in the world. Workers face many dangers every day. These dangers include electricity, chemicals, fire, fumes, heavy machines, and working at high places. Any of these can cause serious injury or even death.

Because of these hazards, safety is the number one priority in every oil and gas company. Every worker must follow the safety rules. Rules protect workers from accidents and injuries.

Personal Protective Equipment — or PPE — is an important part of safety. PPE includes hard hats, safety boots, goggles, gloves, ear protectors, and face guards. A worker must always wear the correct PPE for the job. For example, a worker handling chemicals must wear a respirator and chemical-resistant gloves.

Safety signs also protect workers. These signs tell workers about hazards nearby, what they must not do, and what they must wear or carry. Workers must know what every sign means.

A toolbox talk is a short safety meeting before work starts. A supervisor talks to the team about the hazards for that day's job. Everyone must listen carefully. "Safety first" is not just a phrase — it is a way of working every day.
