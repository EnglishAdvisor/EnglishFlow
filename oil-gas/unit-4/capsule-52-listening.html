Toolbox talk — Crane and lifting safety

Supervisor briefing on lifting hazards — listen twice.

A toolbox talk is a short safety briefing before work starts. The supervisor talks about the hazards for that day and the rules workers must follow. Today the talk is about crane and lifting operations.

1Complete the safety rules — listen and write the missing words
1. Never stand under the load while the crane is lifting.
2. Never try to stop a swinging load with your hands or body.
3. Always watch where you put your hands — they can get caught in the sling.
4. Always have radio contact with the crane operator before and during the lift.
5. Check the weight of the load. Never lift more than the crane's maximum load.
2Emergency hand signal
✋
Emergency stop: Open hand raised, palm facing outwards. Move hand from side to side. This signal means: STOP EVERYTHING NOW.

Toolbox talk vocabulary
toolbox talk — safety briefing
swinging load — load moving like a pendulum
hand signal — body language for cranes
stand back — move away from danger
Grammar note
Rules use Never + infinitive or Always + infinitive:
"Never stand under the load."
"Always check the weight."
