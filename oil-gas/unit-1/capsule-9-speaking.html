Speaking — Checking information

In the oil and gas industry, checking information is critical. Listen to the model conversation, then practise with a partner.

Model conversation — Checking a delivery
Worker A:Excuse me. Are you the delivery driver?
Worker B:Yes, that's right.
Worker A:Can I check the reference number, please?
Worker B:Sure. It's seven-nine-three-four-two-one.
Worker A:Sorry, can you repeat that? Seven-nine…?
Worker B:Seven-nine-three-four-two-one.
Worker A:So that's 793421. Is that right?
Worker B:Yes, that's correct.
Worker A:And the quantity? How many boxes?
Worker B:Forty-eight boxes.
Worker A:Forty-eight. OK, thank you.
Useful phrases for checking
Asking to check
Can I check...?
Can you confirm...?
What is the reference number?
Asking to repeat
Sorry, can you repeat that?
Could you say that again?
Slowly, please.
Reading back
So that's..., is that right?
Let me read that back...
Confirming
Yes, that's correct.
That's right.
No, it's actually...
Activity — Practise and record

With a partner (or alone, playing both roles), practise a similar checking conversation. Use these details:

  1. Reference: 485012 — 25 drums of lubricant
  2. Reference: 061739 — 130 metres of pipe

Remember to check, repeat, and confirm!
