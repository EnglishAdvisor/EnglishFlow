Reading — Who can employ you in oil and gas?

Four main types of company employ people in the oil and gas industry. Where do you fit?

A National Oil Company (NOC) belongs to the government of a country. In Brazil, the NOC is Petrobras — one of the biggest deep-water operators in the world. Norway has Equinor; Saudi Arabia has Aramco. Not every country has one.

Operating companies search for oil and gas, and produce it. They hold licences to explore an area, and they are responsible for the project. In Brazil, international operators like Shell, TotalEnergies, and Equinor work side by side with Petrobras — they often form partnerships to share the costs and the risks of deep water.

Drilling companies do one thing, and do it well: they drill the wells. They do not own the oil — they provide a service to the operating company, with their own rigs and crews.

Service companies do everything else the operation needs: seismic surveys, well logging, maintenance, catering, helicopters, logistics. Global names like SLB, Baker Hughes, and Halliburton — and hundreds of smaller companies — keep every platform running.

And where do people find these jobs in Brazil? On company websites, on LinkedIn, and through agencies in cities like Macaé — the capital of the offshore industry.

NOC
Government-owned. Controls national oil resources.
e.g. Aramco, Equinor, Petrobras
Operating company
Searches for and produces oil and gas. Holds licences.
e.g. BP, Shell, ExxonMobil
Drilling company
Drills wells for operating companies. Service provider.
e.g. Transocean, Halliburton
Service company
All other technical services — surveys to logistics.
e.g. SLB, Baker Hughes
