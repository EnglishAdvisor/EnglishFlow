Writing Bank 1 — Notes
What is a note?
Notes are short informal written messages. They are used between colleagues to communicate quickly. Notes often use abbreviations to save time.

Key features of notes:
  • Short — only essential information
  • Use abbreviations and telegraphic style
  • Include: To, From, Date, Subject, Message
  • Informal but clear
Common abbreviations in notes
JulJuly (any month)
rtnreturn
plsplease
asapas soon as possible
a.m.before noon
p.m.after noon
infoinformation
approx.approximately
dept.department
ref.reference
tel.telephone
w/with
Activity 1 — Match the two halves of each note

Match the beginning (1–4) with the correct ending (a–d):

  1. "Pls rtn the ref. documents to the office"
  2. "Meeting rescheduled to 10 a.m."
  3. "Parts arrived — approx. 50 bolts."
  4. "Pls send info to the safety dept."

Endings:

  1. Check qty and rtn form asap.
  2. Pls confirm attendance asap.
  3. Ref. no. attached — keep for records.
  4. Asap — before 5 p.m. today.
