Global positioning — key GPS terms
GPS
Global Positioning System — tells you your exact position on Earth
Satellite
object orbiting Earth that sends signals to GPS receivers
Signal
radio wave sent from satellite to receiver
Receiver
the GPS device that picks up satellite signals
Latitude (LAT)
distance north or south of the equator in degrees
equator = 0°, north pole = 90° N
Longitude (LON)
distance east or west of Greenwich in degrees
Greenwich = 0°
Coordinates
two numbers (lat + lon) that fix a position on Earth
18.5415° / 54.9220°
Waypoint
a predetermined position on a route — target location

GPS vocabulary
GPS — Global Positioning System
sat nav — satellite navigation device
latitude — north/south position in degrees
longitude — east/west position in degrees
coordinates — lat + lon numbers together
bearing — clockwise angle from north (correct direction)
heading — direction you are currently pointing
waypoint — target position on your route
How to say numbers
322° = three hundred and twenty-two degrees
18.5415° = eighteen point five four one five
0046 m = forty-six metres
