1Complete the notes — How to use a GPS receiver
1
Switch on the GPS receiver.
✓ on
2
The receiver picks up / searches for signals from satellites.
✓ picks up / searches for
3
It uses signals from three or more satellites to calculate your position.
✓ three (minimum for triangulation)
4
Your position is shown as coordinates — latitude and longitude.
✓ coordinates
5
Enter your waypoints — the positions you want to reach.
✓ waypoints
6
The GPS shows your heading and the bearing to the next waypoint.
✓ heading · bearing
7
Adjust your direction so your heading matches the bearing.
✓ matches
8
When you arrive at a waypoint, the GPS beeps and moves to the next.
✓ arrive
2Answer the questions about GPS
1
How many satellite signals does a GPS need? — At least three.
2
What does GPS stand for? — Global Positioning System.
3
What is the difference between heading and bearing? — Heading = direction you are going now. Bearing = correct direction to the waypoint.

GPS listening vocab
switch on/off — ligar / desligar
pick up signals — captar sinais
calculate — calcular
triangulation — calculating position from 3 points
coordinates — lat + lon numbers
waypoint — target position
heading — current direction
bearing — correct direction
adjust — change to the correct direction
arrive at — reach a point
How GPS works
1 Receiver picks up ≥3 satellite signals
2 Calculates coordinates (lat/lon)
3 User enters waypoints
4 Device shows heading vs bearing
5 User adjusts direction
6 Device beeps at each waypoint
