Writing sentences — rules for punctuation
Capital letter
Every sentence starts with a capital letter.
Sentences always begin...
Full stop (.)
Statements end with a full stop.
He works here.
Question mark (?)
Questions end with a question mark.
Where is he?
Exclamation (!)
Imperatives can use ! or full stop.
Stop! / Stop.
Also remember: Names of people and places always start with a capital letter. Example: Faisal, Saudi Arabia, the North Sea.

Sentences always begin with a capital letter. Statements always have a full stop at the end. Questions have a question mark. Imperatives have a full stop or sometimes an exclamation mark. Punctuation marks
. full stop — end of statement (BrE)
. period — end of statement (AmE)
? question mark — end of question
! exclamation mark — strong feeling or imperative
, comma — pause within a sentence
capital letter — A, B, C (uppercase)
lower case — a, b, c (small letters)
Sentence types
Statement: ends with .
Question: ends with ?
Imperative: ends with . or !
All: start with capital
