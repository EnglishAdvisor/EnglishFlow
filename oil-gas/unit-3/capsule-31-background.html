Background — The downstream sector

The oil and gas industry has two main sectors: the upstream sector and the downstream sector. The downstream sector processes crude oil and natural gas into useful products for everyday life.

Refineries are the key facilities in the downstream sector. They receive crude oil from pipelines or tankers and separate it into light and heavy products. Light products include petrol (gasoline), diesel, and jet fuel. Heavy products include asphalt — the material used to build roads. Refineries also produce propane, a gas used in homes and industry.

The petrochemical industry is also part of downstream. Petrochemical plants take hydrocarbons from refineries and produce chemicals. Industries use these chemicals to make plastics, detergents, fertilizers, and synthetic rubber for car tyres.

Gas processing plants separate natural gas into different gases such as methane, ethane, and propane. Methane is the main component of natural gas for heating and cooking. Ethylene is an important chemical for the plastics industry.

Products reach consumers by sea in large tankers, by road in petrol tankers, by rail, and by pipeline. Control rooms monitor and control the whole process 24 hours a day, seven days a week.
