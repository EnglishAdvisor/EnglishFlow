Phone messages — changing pronouns

When you write a phone message for someone, you change the pronouns:
The caller says "I" → you write "he/she"  |  The caller says "you" → you write "him/her"

1Messages for George (Technical Support)

Change the pronouns. Write the corrected message:

Original (what caller said)
"I'm calling about the new computers. Can you call me back, please? I want to check the delivery date. My number is 4521."
Message to write for George
From: Andrew Watts, HR
Message: He's calling about the new computers. Can you call him back? He wants to check the delivery date. His number is 4521.
2Messages for Faisal Hamdi (Human Resources)

Read each caller's words. Write the message. Change the pronouns.

Caller 1 — Omar, Control Room
"I need to speak to Faisal about my new work schedule. Can you ask him to call me? My extension is 2134."
Caller 2 — Sara, Training Dept.
"I'm calling because I want to update my personal file. Can Faisal call me before three o'clock? My number is 3310."
Caller 3 — Jean-Claude, Maintenance
"I'm applying for the night-shift supervisor position. I sent my application last week. Can you confirm you received it?"
Caller 4 — Group task
Write your own message from an imaginary caller. Use the same format.

✏️ Write the messages for Faisal (Activity 2 — at least callers 1 and 2). Send via WhatsApp.

Pronoun changes
I → he / she
me → him / her
my → his / her
you → him / her
your → his / her
Message format
From: [name, dept]
Message: [what he/she wants]
Number: [ext / phone]
Sample — Caller 1
From: Omar, Control Room
Message: He needs to speak about his new work schedule. Can you call him? His ext. is 2134.
