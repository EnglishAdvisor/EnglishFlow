News items — Read both articles
A — Petrochemicals: a changing world
The petrochemical industry is changing. Companies are moving production to places with cheaper energy. Saudi Arabia and China are building large new plants. Europe and the US have older plants and higher costs.

Some businesses are closing plants in Europe and opening new ones in Asia and the Middle East. They are investing millions of dollars in new technology. This is creating problems for workers in Europe who are losing their jobs.

Countries like Qatar and the UAE are building plants close to their oil and gas fields. This means they can produce petrochemicals more cheaply.

Analysts say the changes are going to continue for many years. "We are seeing a new map of the petrochemical world," said one industry expert.
B — Gas: going up
The demand for natural gas is increasing around the world. Many countries are building new gas-fired power stations because gas produces less carbon than coal or oil.

In Asia, countries are planning to double their use of natural gas in the next ten years. Japan and South Korea are importing more Liquefied Natural Gas (LNG).

Gas companies are building new LNG facilities to meet this demand. Engineers are currently working on several large projects in Australia and Qatar.

Experts say that gas is going to play an important role in the future energy mix. "We are moving towards cleaner energy, and gas is a key part of this story," said a spokesperson.
