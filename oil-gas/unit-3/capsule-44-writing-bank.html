Writing Bank 3 — An email reporting an IT problem

Learn the 5-part structure. Each part has a key phrase to learn.

Part 1 — Greeting
Dear IT Support,
Use the department name if you don't know the person's name.
Part 2 — State equipment
I am writing about [the computer / printer / screen] in [location].
Be specific: give the equipment name and where it is.
Part 3 — Describe the problem
The [equipment] is [not working / showing an error / making a noise].
Use Present Continuous for what is happening now.
Part 4 — Request
Could you please send someone to fix it? / Could you advise me?
Use "Could you please…?" for polite requests.
Part 5 — Close
Thank you for your help.
Then: Best regards, / [Your name] / [Department]
Model email
To: IT Support
From: Jang Li, Control Room
Subject: Computer problem — Control Room
Dear IT Support,

I am writing about the computer on the main control panel in the Control Room on Level 2.

The screen is showing an error message and the keyboard is not responding. The computer has been like this since the start of my shift.

Could you please send someone to look at it as soon as possible? We are using a hand-held computer at the moment, but this is not ideal.

Thank you for your help.

Best regards,
Jang Li
Control Room Operator

✏️ Write your own email to IT Support about a problem with equipment. Use the 5-part structure. Send via WhatsApp.

Writing tips
Subject line — always include; say what it's about in 6 words or less
Be specific — name the equipment and location
Polite language — use "Could you please…?" not "Fix this!"
Present Continuous — "The screen is showing an error"
Sign off — name + job title + department
Useful phrases
"I am writing about…"
"The [equipment] is not working."
"Could you please…?"
"Thank you for your help."
"Best regards,"
