About Suparman

"I work at an oil refinery in Sumatra, Indonesia. It's a big refinery. There are more than 300 tanks and nearly 1,000 workers. My team takes care of all of the refinery equipment.

When the refinery is running normally, we monitor all of the equipment. This means we check and maintain everything. When there is a problem, we troubleshoot it. That means trying to understand what's wrong. Then we try to repair it. We work closely with the maintenance team.

Sometimes, we need to shut down part of the refinery for a big repair job and for some maintenance jobs, for example furnace cleaning. Shutting down means safely stopping some of the machines. But you can't just switch it off! We usually schedule a maintenance shut down a year in advance. We work closely with an experienced, specialist contractor.

Another important job is checking the products that are made in the refinery. This means we test the petrol, the kerosene, and so on to make sure it is good. My team also writes in the production log."

Activity 2 — Comprehension questions
1. How many people work at Suparman's refinery?
Nearly 1,000 workers.
2. What two reasons does he give for shutting down?
Big repair jobs and some maintenance jobs (e.g. furnace cleaning).
3. What maintenance job does he mention?
Furnace cleaning.
4. How much time do they take to plan a shut down?
About a year in advance.
5. What two refinery products does he mention?
Petrol and kerosene.
