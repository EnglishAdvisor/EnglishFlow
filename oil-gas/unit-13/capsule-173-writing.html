Activity — Write a paragraph explaining a process

A paragraph is a group of sentences that explain one idea. Choose one process from the list on the right and write a paragraph of 5–8 sentences. Use sequence words and passive verbs.

Model paragraph — How fractional distillation works
First, the crude oil is pumped into the furnace, where it is heated to about 400 degrees Celsius. Next, the boiling oil enters the bottom of the distillation tower. Inside the tower, the crude oil is separated into fractions — parts with different boiling points. The lightest product, petroleum gas, rises to the top of the tower. The heaviest products, like asphalt and fuel oil, sink to the bottom. After that, each fraction is piped out of the tower at its own level. The products are then stored in large tanks inside the refinery. Finally, they are taken out of the refinery by tanker lorry, rail tanker, boat, or pipeline.

Writing checklist
Your paragraph should include:
✔ First, Next, After that, Finally

✔ 5–8 sentences
✔ One clear idea per sentence
