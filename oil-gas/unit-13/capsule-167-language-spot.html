The Passive — Present Simple
We use Passive verbs to explain actions or processes — especially when we focus on what happens to the object, not who does it.
The crude oil is refined in the refinery. (Passive)
The pipes take the oil from the ships. (Active)
The oil is taken from the ships by the pipes. (Passive)
Form: subject + is/are + past participle (+ by + agent, optional)
Subjectis/arePast participleExample
The oilisrefinedThe oil is refined here.
ProductsarestoredProducts are stored in tanks.
The treeshide—Active: Trees hide the refinery.
