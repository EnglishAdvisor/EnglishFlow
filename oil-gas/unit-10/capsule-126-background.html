Oil and the Environment

The oil industry has an environmental impact at every stage — from exploration and development to decommissioning. The world consumes about 90 million barrels of oil every day. Companies must plan carefully to minimise environmental damage.

When working in desert areas, companies must protect desert plants and animals. In arctic conditions, engineers must design equipment that works in extreme cold. When drilling near towns and villages, companies must think about traffic, noise, and bad smells.

At every stage, oil companies work to:

  • minimise flaring of natural gas
  • treat and recycle cooling water before returning it to rivers or the sea
  • develop wildlife protection programmes
  • use special drilling techniques to protect the environment

Many oil companies are now also investing in sustainable and renewable energy to prepare for a future with less oil. From planning through to decommissioning, protecting the environment is part of every major project.

Key Terms
▸environmental impact — the effect of human activity on nature
▸decommissioning — safely closing down an oil facility at the end of its life
▸flaring — burning off unwanted gas into the atmosphere
▸cooling water — water used to cool equipment and processes
▸wildlife protection — programmes to keep animals and plants safe during operations
▸sustainable energy — energy that can be used without running out
▸renewable energy — energy from sources that are naturally replenished (sun, wind)
▸nature reserve — an area where plants and animals are protected
▸eco-hazard — something that can harm the environment
▸extended reach drilling — drilling at an angle to reach oil far from the rig
