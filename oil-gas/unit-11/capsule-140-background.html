Workshop Operations

Oil and gas facilities — both onshore and offshore — have a workshop: a dedicated space where skilled maintenance technicians service, repair, and manufacture parts. Workshop specializations include mechanical, piping, electrical, control and instruments, and computer and communications support.

While much work happens in the field — at the location of the equipment — jobs that need specialized tools, better environmental conditions, or high precision are brought into the workshop. The typical workshop has a lathe, a milling machine, a grinder, gas and arc welding equipment, electrical test equipment, and portable hand tools.

Good organization is essential. Work is tracked using job cards — documents that describe the task, list required materials, confirm scheduling, and show that a risk assessment and permit to work are in place. Job cards are prioritized and scheduled by the workshop manager.

Safety zones are marked around large tools. Only the person using the tool may enter the zone; others stay outside the line. PPE — hard hat, goggles, gloves, and safety shoes — must always be used. Dust and fume extraction systems are installed to keep the air clean.

Precision measurement is critical in workshop operations. Modern CNC machines can work to tolerances of ±25 microns — far narrower than a human hair. Metric and imperial units can both appear in older facilities, so workers must always check which system is being used.

Record-keeping is also important. Maintenance records build an equipment history, making it easier to plan future maintenance and reduce costly breakdowns.

job carda document describing a task, its materials, schedule, and safety requirements

tolerancethe allowable variation in a measurement; CNC machines achieve ±25 microns

oxyacetyleneoxygen + acetylene gas mix used for high-temperature cutting and welding

lathea machine that shapes metal or wood by rotating it against a cutting tool

maintenance techniciana skilled worker who inspects, services, and repairs equipment
