Present Perfect
We use the Present Perfect to talk about recent actions and to say what has (or hasn't) been done.
Recent actions
AHave you finished the work on the compressor?
BWe've replaced the gaskets, but we haven't put the new bearings in.
AHas Ahmed phoned?
BNo, he hasn't.
Life experience
AHave you ever used a drill press?
BNo, I've never used one. Have you?
AYes, I have.

Structure
Positive: I/we/they have + past participle
He/she/it has + past participle
Negative: I haven't / He hasn't + past participle
Question: Have you / Has he + past participle?
Short answers: Yes, I have. / No, he hasn't.
