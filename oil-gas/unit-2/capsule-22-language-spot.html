Language Spot — do / does & Wh- questions
do / does — present simple questions
SubjectAuxiliaryExample
I / you / we / theydoDo you work in the upstream sector?
he / she / itdoesDoes Steve record data?
Short answers: Yes, I do. / No, she doesn't.
Negative: I don't work... / She doesn't start...
Wh- questions
How
manner, means
Which
choice from a group
Where
place
Who
person
When
time
Why
reason
What
thing, action
Structure: Wh- word + do/does + subject + verb?
Example: Where does Steve work? → In Canada.
Activity 1 — Choose do or does
1. _______ scientists study rocks?
2. _______ the driller work 12-hour shifts?
3. _______ you work in the upstream sector?
4. _______ roughnecks help the driller?
5. _______ a geologist decide where to drill?
Activity 2 — Write a Wh- question for each answer
1. A: Steve works in Canada.  →  Q: _______ does Steve work?
2. A: He records pressure and temperature.  →  Q: _______ does he record?
3. A: He works for a service company.  →  Q: _______ company does he work for?
4. A: He works 12 hours.  →  Q: _______ long does he work?
5. A: The engineers study the data.  →  Q: _______ studies the data?
