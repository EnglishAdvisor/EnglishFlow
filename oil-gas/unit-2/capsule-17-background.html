Background — The upstream sector

The oil and gas industry has two main sectors: the upstream sector and the downstream sector.

Workers in the upstream sector find and produce crude oil and gas. Crude oil is oil that comes directly from the ground — it is usually dark brown in colour. Workers in the downstream sector produce useful products from crude oil and natural gas, such as fuel for cars and planes.

The upstream process has four main steps. First, exploration: scientists study rocks and do scientific tests to find places where hydrocarbons may be present. Hydrocarbons are compounds made of hydrogen and carbon — oil and gas are both hydrocarbons.

The second step is drilling. Drillers drill a well in a promising location. They do not always find hydrocarbons. If they do, the company then carries out more tests. This leads to the third step: development. The company prepares for full production — for example, by building a pipeline to transport the oil or gas to market. Development can take a few weeks or many years.

Finally, production begins. Crude oil and gas flow from the well and along the pipeline. The upstream sector is often called the E&P sector — exploration and production.
