Activity 1 — Tap a card to reveal what the product is used for
1
adhesives
Glues and bonding agents. Used in construction, packaging, shoes, and electronics. Many are made from synthetic resins derived from petroleum.
2
fertilizer
Chemicals that help plants grow. Ammonia — a key fertilizer ingredient — is made using natural gas. It feeds crops that produce most of the world's food.
3
paints
Synthetic paints contain resins, solvents, and pigments — all from petrochemical sources. They are more durable and consistent than traditional plant-based paints.
4
plastics
Lightweight and versatile synthetic materials. Used in packaging, pipes, vehicles, electronics, medical devices, and thousands of other applications.
5
carpeting
Most carpet fibres (nylon, polypropylene, polyester) come from petrochemicals. Synthetic fibres are durable, stain-resistant, and less expensive than wool.
6
cosmetics
Petrochemical ingredients appear in shampoo, lipstick, moisturiser, and perfume. They act as emollients, preservatives, and texture-improving agents.
7
rubber
Synthetic rubber (from butadiene) is used in tyres, hoses, seals, and gloves. It is more resistant to heat and chemicals than natural rubber from trees.
8
medications
Many drugs, tablets, and capsules contain active ingredients or coatings derived from petrochemicals. Aspirin, antibiotics, and vitamins rely on synthetic chemistry.
9
clothes
Polyester, nylon, acrylic, and spandex are all synthetic fibres from petrochemicals. They are used in sportswear, uniforms, workwear, and everyday clothing.
