and · but · because
and
Joins two sentences to add information.
It was discovered in prehistoric times, and it's commonly used today.
but
Joins two sentences when the second is unexpected or contrasts the first.
They are very useful, but they also have some problems.
Carbon black wasn't a new product, but using a factory was a new way of making it.
because
Joins two sentences to show reason (why).
It became possible to make large amounts cheaply because natural gas was plentiful.
People throw away a lot of plastic products because they are inexpensive.
Activity 1 — Choose and, but, or because
1I was late this morning because I had a problem with my car.
2We repaired the compressor yesterday, but it stopped working again this morning.
3We started work at 9.00, and we finished at 4.00.
4I want to go to the meeting, but I really don't have time.
5We'll tidy up the workshop today and start work on the pump repair tomorrow.
6I can't replace the lamp today because I haven't got a new one.
Activity 2 — Write your own sentences

Join these ideas using and, but, or because. More than one answer may be correct.

1We called the medic. / Juan injured his hand. / I cut my finger. / It wasn't serious.
2We replaced the gasket. / We cleaned the spark plug. / It's running much better. / It's making a funny noise.
3We aren't using this tank. / It's damaged. / We're using the other tank. / It isn't damaged.
Quick guide
and = additionadds more information (both facts are equal)
but = contrastsecond fact is surprising or different
because = reasonexplains WHY something happened
