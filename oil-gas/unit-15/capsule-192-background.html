Every time you put on a nylon jacket, take a medicine, or drive a car, you are using petrochemicals. Petrochemicals are chemicals made from petroleum and petroleum gas. They are found in thousands of everyday products — from plastics and fertilizers to cosmetics, medications, and rubber.

The petrochemical industry began in 1872, when the first petrochemical factory was built to make carbon black from natural gas. In the early 1900s, oil refineries produced large amounts of chemical by-products, and companies began to find ways to use them. From the 1920s to the 1940s, scientists developed familiar synthetic materials — nylon, polystyrene, and PVC. These new materials were strong, cheap, and easy to produce in large quantities.

Today, petrochemicals are essential to modern life. However, they also present challenges. Many plastic products do not rot or break down like natural materials. Plastic waste is polluting the world's oceans and harming wildlife. Scientists and manufacturers continue to work on developing safer and more sustainable petrochemical products for the future.

In Unit 15, you will explore the history of petrochemicals, study key grammar (and, but, because), learn to describe a petrochemical factory and its products, and write a materials requisition form.
