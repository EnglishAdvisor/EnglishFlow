if / when / in case — how to use them
if / when
Use for expected situations — things that will probably happen.
"When someone is sick or injured, I take care of them."
"If someone is badly injured, I arrange evacuation."
in case
Use for plans and preparations for possible emergencies.
"We have an evacuation plan in case we need to get everyone off the rig."
"I always carry my mobile phone in case there's an emergency."

Quick Guide
if / when + presentexpected situations: "If it rains, I take an umbrella."
in case + presentpreparation for possibility: "I take an umbrella in case it rains."
if vs whenif = maybe; when = will definitely happen
Examples (oil & gas)
When the alarm rings …→ it always/definitely happens
If there's a leak …→ it might happen
… in case there's a fire→ preparing for a possibility
We have SCBA in case …… the air becomes toxic
