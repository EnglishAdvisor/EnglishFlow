Joao speaks about his job

"I'm a welder and I work closely with the pipe-fitters. Before I start welding, the pipe-fitters do a lot of preparation work. They read the plans, cut and prepare the pipes to the right length, lay them out, drill holes for instruments, and assemble the flanges, elbows, and tees using bolts.

After the pipe-fitters have finished their preparation, I weld the pipes together. Welding joins the metal permanently using a very high temperature. After I have finished welding, the pipe-fitters assemble the completed sections, and then the inspectors inspect and test the pipes.

The main hazards in my job are electric shock, burns from the hot metal and the arc rays, and breathing problems from the smoke. I always wear my protective equipment — helmet, leather gloves, and leather shoes — and I make sure the area is well ventilated before I start."
