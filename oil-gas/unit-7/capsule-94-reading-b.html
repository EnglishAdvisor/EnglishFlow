Isometrics and MTOs

An isometric drawing shows a three-dimensional view of a piping system on flat paper. It shows the direction and orientation of pipes without using perspective — the drawing makes it easy to understand a complex piping system in three dimensions.

Engineers use the isometric to prepare a Material Take Off (MTO). The MTO is a complete list of all the materials and components that are needed to build the piping system: pipe sections, elbows, tees, flanges, valves, bolts, and gaskets. Each item in the MTO has a size, a description, and a quantity.

The MTO is used to order the materials from the supplier. The fabricator then receives the materials and makes pipe spools in the workshop. Each spool is a section of pipe with its fittings already welded or assembled, ready for installation on site.

Key terms
isometric — 3D pipe drawing on flat paper
MTO — Material Take Off (parts list)
pipe spool — prefabricated pipe section
SCH40 / SCH80 — pipe wall thickness (schedule)
fabricator — makes pipe spools in workshop
supplier — provides materials
quantity — number of items needed
flanged — connected with flanges
MTO process
1 Engineer draws isometric
2 MTO prepared from isometric
3 Materials ordered from supplier
4 Fabricator makes pipe spools
5 Spools installed on site
