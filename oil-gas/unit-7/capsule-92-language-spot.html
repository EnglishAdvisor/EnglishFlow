Grammar rule — Countable vs Uncountable nouns
Key differences
Countable (C)
  • You can count them
  • Have singular & plural
  • Use: a, the, some, many
  • a cylinder / cylinders
  • How many cylinders?
Uncountable (U)
  • You cannot count them
  • No plural form
  • Use: some, much (not a)
  • some smoke / smoke
  • How much smoke?
⚠️ Important: equipment, smoke, water, oil, information are UNCOUNTABLE in English.
1Write C (countable) or U (uncountable)
steamU

Countable examples
cylinder(s) — can count: 3 cylinders
spark(s) — can count: 10 sparks
deposit(s) — can count: many deposits
brush(es) — can count: 2 brushes
welder(s) — can count: 5 welders
Uncountable examples
smoke — NOT: smokes / a smoke
water — NOT: waters / a water
oil — NOT: oils / an oil
equipment — NOT: equipments
information — NOT: informations
Quantifiers
C: a, some, any, many, few
U: some, any, much, little
Both: a lot of, no
