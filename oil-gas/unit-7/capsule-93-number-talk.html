Pipe measurements — dimensions
D
Outside diameter
full outer width of pipe
B
Bore
inner opening (= 2r)
S
Wall thickness
thickness of pipe wall
L
Length
length of the pipe
r
Inside radius
half of the bore (B ÷ 2)
C
Circumference
distance around the pipe
Volume formula
Volume of a cylinder (pipe)
V = π × r² × L
V = volume (m³) | π = 3.14 | r = inside radius (m) | L = length (m)
Worked example
Pipe: L = 12 m, bore = 1 m (so r = 0.5 m)
V = 3.14 × 0.5 × 0.5 × 12
V = 3.14 × 0.25 × 12
V = 3.14 × 3 = 9.42 m³

✓ V = 3.14 × 0.2² × 10 = 3.14 × 0.04 × 10 = 1.26 m³
Pipe C: L = 5 m, bore = 1.0 m (r = 0.5 m). What is the volume?
✓ V = 3.14 × 0.5² × 5 = 3.14 × 0.25 × 5 = 3.93 m³

Key measurements
diameter — full width across
radius — half the diameter
bore — inner opening of pipe
wall thickness — pipe material width
circumference — distance around outside
volume — space inside pipe
Quick reference
r = B ÷ 2
D = B + (2 × S)
C = π × D
V = π × r² × L
π = 3.14
Remember
Use metres (m) for large pipes, centimetres (cm) for smaller pipes. Don't mix units in calculations.
