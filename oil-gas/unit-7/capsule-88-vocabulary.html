Arc welding — how it works

In arc welding, electricity makes an arc — a very bright light between two metals. The arc is extremely hot and melts the metal, joining two pieces together permanently.

Arc welding creates several hazards that welders must understand and control.

⚡
Electric shock
Can cause burns or death. Never weld in wet conditions.
🔥
Arc rays
Invisible UV rays burn skin and eyes. Always use a welder's helmet.
✨
Arc sparks
Hot sparks can cause fires. Remove flammable items before welding.
💨
Smoke / fumes
Hurt eyes, nose, mouth and cause breathing problems. Use ventilation.
Protection equipment

Welders use leather protective clothing because leather is very strong, doesn't melt easily, and is resistant to heat. Leather comes from animal skin.

⛑️
Helmet
protects eyes & head from arc rays
🥾
Leather shoes
protect feet from sparks & falls
🧤
Leather gloves
protect hands from heat & electric shock
