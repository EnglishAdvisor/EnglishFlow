Writing tips for a service report description
1. Say what is wrong (what doesn't work?)
2. Say what you found (what was the problem?)
3. Say what you did (what repair steps did you take?)
4. Say whether it is solved (is it fixed? any follow-up?)
Use this scenario: A 2" pipe connection at the pump outlet was leaking. You found the threads were worn and replaced the elbow fitting. After repair you tested with water. No leaks. Work completed.

Report structure
Header: date, site, client, contact, technician
Times: start time, finish time
Description: problem → diagnosis → action → result
Signature: technician's name
Useful verbs
identified — found the problem
replaced — put in a new part
retightened — tightened again
pressurised — filled with pressure
tested — checked for leaks
confirmed — verified the result
Model phrases
"The ___ was leaking at the ___."
"I replaced the ___ and retightened the bolts."
"I tested by pressurising with water."
"There were no further leaks."
"The ___ is now fully operational."
