Vocabulary — Electricity & Circuits · Unit 8

Ohm's Law — The three key electrical equations:

V Voltage (V) = Current (I) × Resistance (R)
I Current (I) = Voltage (V) ÷ Resistance (R)
R Resistance (R) = Voltage (V) ÷ Current (I)
Current (I)Flow of electrons through a conductor — measured in Amperes (A)
Voltage (V)Electrical pressure that pushes current — measured in Volts (V)
Resistance (R)Opposition to current flow — measured in Ohms (Ω)
CircuitClosed loop that allows current to flow from source and back
MultimeterInstrument that measures voltage, current and resistance
Short CircuitUnintended low-resistance path — causes excess current & danger
